About

I am an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science Department of Bucknell University. I am passionate about inclusive STEM education, the mathematical foundations of computer science, and youth outreach. I used to be an Assistant Professor in the Mathematics and Computer Science Department of St. Mary's College of California.

I completed my PhD in 2023 as a part of the PPLV group in the Computer Science Department of University College London. My supervisers were the superstars Alexandra Silva and Jurriaan Rot. My PhD thesis is titled Coalgebraic Completeness Theorems for Effectful Process Algebras, and offers a general framework for axiomatizing process algebras that consist of action sequencing operators, an algebraic effect, and recursion. One of the main contributions of the framework, I would say, is how it handles unguarded recursive specifications. The work generalizes the completeness theorem for Guarded Kleene Algebra with Tests, or GKAT, from (Smolka et al, 2019). Effectful process algebras are closely connected but orthogonal to the calculi studied in Alexandra's PhD thesis.

Interests
I am generally interested in universal algebra and coalgebra, iteration theory, and process algebra, but on occasion I am willing to admit to my obession with generative systems of the form \(X \to MFX\), where \(M\) is a monad of some kind and \(F\) is some polynomial functor. I spend a lot of time thinking about monad presentations and final coalgebras. Recently, I have been working with behavioural differential equations, algebraic approaches to trace semantics, and lattices. I used to work with fractals and generative music. I still do, but I used to too.
